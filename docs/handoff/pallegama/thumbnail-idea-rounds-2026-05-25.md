# Pallegama Thumbnail Idea Rounds

Date: 2026-05-25  
Status: working visual options, not final editorial clearance

## Round 1 - Direction Generation

1. **Dark Sermon Portrait**
   - Use the monk image as a dimmed full-bleed background.
   - Make the main message spiritual and civic: sacred power must face law.
   - Keep allegation status visible so the thumbnail does not convict by design.

2. **Evidence Board Over Portrait**
   - Keep the existing Analyst evidence-board style: source cards, red/green threads, compact status chips.
   - Treat the image as context, behind the evidence apparatus.
   - Emphasize records still required: complaint, NCPA letter, B reports, court minutes.

3. **Court Process Ledger**
   - Build a stricter process thumbnail: timeline bars, status stamps, missing-record marks.
   - Use the portrait cropped to one side, not centered as a dramatic accusation.
   - The visual message: this case now turns on records, dates, and institutional conduct.

4. **Civic Fracture Map**
   - Map power nodes around the portrait: police, AG, Sangha office, media, child protection, public outrage.
   - Use a darker palette and Analyst map language.
   - Avoid sensational child imagery or guilt-coded red over the face.

## Round 2 - Selection Refinement

1. **Dark Sermon Portrait**
   - Strongest for article hero/social share.
   - Risk: can become too poster-like if the face is too bright.
   - Refinement: heavy dark veil, sermon title in cream/saffron, allegation-status chip.

2. **Evidence Board Over Portrait**
   - Strongest for Analyst continuity with Mullivaikkal evidence-board thumbnails.
   - Risk: may feel busy at small sizes.
   - Refinement: fewer cards, thicker title block, only four source labels.

3. **Court Process Ledger**
   - Strongest for legal-process seriousness and due-process posture.
   - Risk: less emotionally powerful.
   - Refinement: large title plus visible timeline dates; portrait is secondary context.

4. **Civic Fracture Map**
   - Strongest for the article thesis: religious legitimacy, state power, and child protection collide.
   - Risk: too conceptual if node labels dominate.
   - Refinement: use six large nodes only; keep the central argument clear.

## Recommendation

Use **Option A - Dark Sermon Portrait** for the first live article thumbnail if the page needs impact. Keep **Option B - Evidence Board Over Portrait** as the safer Analyst-style alternative for contexts where due-process restraint matters more than immediate social reach.
