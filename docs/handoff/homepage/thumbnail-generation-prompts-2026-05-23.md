# Analyst Thumbnail Prompt Queue - 2026-05-23

## Operating Standard

Use the accepted Analyst dossier thumbnail language: restrained editorial board, evidence surfaces, article screenshots, pins, and connecting threads. The image should feel like a civic investigation, not a conspiracy poster. Keep one clear title block, limited colours, and enough whitespace for small-card readability on the homepage.

## Round 1 - Ridma Lens

The homepage card must read first as an Analyst publication, second as a specific dossier, and third as a method. The repeated visual system is useful: screenshots, pins, thread routes, and a calm centre title. Avoid too many labels. The thumbnail should still work when reduced to a narrow feed card.

## Round 2 - Ravana Lens

The image should carry civic seriousness without turning into accusation theatre. For corruption dossiers, show chains of accountability and public cost, not caricature. Evidence should dominate: documents, procurement routes, energy dependency, and political responsibility.

## Next Article Prompt: Galanguru Coal Corruption

Create two horizontal 16:9 thumbnail options for an Analyst dossier titled:

`The Galanguru Files`

Subtitle:

`Coal, corruption and cascading crises`

Visual direction:

An editorial evidence-board composition on a muted cream or charcoal-cream Analyst background. Use scattered article-page screenshots, procurement document fragments, coal shipment/port silhouettes, invoice-like paper slips, and three pinned route labels: `Coal procurement`, `Accountability chain`, and `Cascading crisis`. Connect every label to a visible pin with restrained thread lines in three colours: muted gold for money/procurement, rust for accountability/conflict, and deep green for public cost/system risk. Keep the title large and clean in the centre-left or lower-left. Add a small `THE ANALYST DOSSIER` mark, but do not let branding overpower the dossier title.

Option A should be lighter, paper-led, and suitable for the homepage feed.

Option B should be slightly darker and more dramatic, with coal/port silhouettes and evidence cards still legible.

Negative constraints:

No cartoon politicians, no fake logos, no heavy conspiracy-board clutter, no unreadable microtext, no excessive red warning styling, no AI-glossy news-studio look.
