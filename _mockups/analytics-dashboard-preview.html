Production Setup Guide
1
Formspree — Email Alerts per Comment
Create a free account at formspree.io. Create a new form. Copy your Form ID (e.g. xrgjabc). Paste it into kalathma-scandal.html replacing YOUR_FORMSPREE_ID. You'll get an email for every comment with full IP, geo, user agent, and text. Free tier = 50/month, paid = unlimited.
const FORMSPREE_ID = 'xrgjabc'; // ← your ID here
2
Supabase — Persistent Comment Storage
Create free account at supabase.com. Create new project. In Table Editor, create table: comments(id uuid, text text, ip text, ua text, geo text, page text, ts timestamptz, approved bool). Enable Row Level Security. Allow insert for anon. Copy Project URL + anon key.
const SUPABASE_URL = 'https://xxxx.supabase.co';
const SUPABASE_KEY = 'eyJh...';
3
Plausible Analytics — Privacy-First Traffic (Recommended)
Plausible.io — $9/month. No cookies. GDPR-compliant. Shows real-time visitor count, referrers, pages. Much better than Google Analytics. Add one script tag to all pages. Dashboard at plausible.io/analyst.rizrazak.com.
<script defer data-domain="analyst.rizrazak.com" src="https://plausible.io/js/plausible.js"></script>
4
Cloudflare — Free Tier Analytics (Already Using CF?)
If you route analyst.rizrazak.com through Cloudflare (via GoDaddy DNS → Cloudflare), you get free analytics: unique visitors, bandwidth, threats blocked, top pages. No script needed — works at the DNS level. Go to dash.cloudflare.com → your domain → Analytics.
5
hCaptcha — Stronger Bot Protection (Optional)
Currently using honeypot + rate limiting. For stronger protection, add hCaptcha (free). Create account at hcaptcha.com. Get site key. Add widget to comment form. Free tier available. More accessible than reCAPTCHA.
<script src="https://js.hcaptcha.com/1/api.js" async defer></script>
<div class="h-captcha" data-sitekey="YOUR_SITE_KEY"></div>
⚖️ Your Legal Exposure — Brief
🚨
Defamation — Highest Risk
Publishing unverified allegations about named individuals is actionable under Sri Lanka's civil defamation law. Burden of proof is on you — you must prove truth, not they must prove falsity. Label everything ALLEGED/UNVERIFIED. Give subjects right of reply. Document your sources privately.
👧
Minor Identification (Kalathma) — Legal Complexity
If Kalathma is genuinely a minor, Sri Lanka's Children and Young Persons Ordinance restricts publishing identifying information about them in legal/quasi-legal contexts. However: framing her sympathetically (victim of suppression) is defensible. Best practice: refer to her by TikTok handle only, no photo, note "understood to be a minor." The moral high ground actually strengthens your story.
🌐
US Hosting (GitHub Pages) — Protective
GitHub's servers are in the US. Under NY Times v. Sullivan (US defamation law), public figures must prove actual malice — knowledge of falsity or reckless disregard for truth. This is a very high bar. SLC officials are public figures. The US standard strongly protects investigative journalism. But Sri Lankan courts may still issue domestic orders.
💬
Comments Section — Platform Liability
As operator of the site, you have partial protection as a platform (analogous to Section 230 in the US) if you didn't write the comments. But if you knowingly leave up defamatory comments, you lose that protection. IP logging shows good faith. Moderate actively. Add clear terms before submission. Consider requiring comments to be approved before display.
🔒
IP Logging — Legal & Prudent
Logging IPs for legal compliance is lawful in most jurisdictions. GDPR note: IPs are personal data under GDPR. If EU users access your site, you need a minimal privacy notice. Consider hashing IPs after 30 days. Tell users clearly that IPs are logged (you've already done this in the terms text) — that's good practice.
💡
Practical Protection — Do This
(1) Never publish anything you don't have ≥2 independent sources for. (2) Keep a private sources log (separate from this site). (3) Always label allegations correctly. (4) Consider anonymous domain registration via a privacy registrar. (5) Route through Cloudflare — their TOS protects you from trivial DDoS attacks. (6) Biggest risk is not losing a case — it's legal costs from fighting even a weak claim. A legal fund / anonymous structure helps.
