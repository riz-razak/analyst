Executive Summary

The $100K Path to Public Launch (12 Months)

WarenYan has a working MVP — real-time ride matching, PostGIS spatial queries, SOS safety system, and full ride lifecycle — running on $0/month infrastructure. The product works. What we need now is the operational runway to take it from a functional demo to a publicly launched, driver-verified, legally registered ride-hailing platform.

The plan compresses the go-to-market into 12 months to public launch. Round 1 ($100K) covers 3 months of intense foundation work: app development kickoff, legal entity formation, driver verification pipeline, rapid driver recruitment (1,000/day peak in Q1), and brand launch preparation. Round 2 ($200-300K) immediately funds months 4-12 for scale-out, marketing blitz, and public launch.

This plan is anchored around a ground operations team from Day 1: PJ (Prageeth Jayathilaka) as DGTL business partner executing on-ground operations, plus 5 Driver Onboarding Agents from Month 1. Together they recruit, verify, and support 1,000+ drivers in Q1 using aggressive, high-touch methods: stand-by-stand blitzes, WhatsApp signup, personal calls, and incentives.

Rapid Driver Recruitment Strategy

1,000/Day Peak in Q1 (Not 12/Day)

The original 12 verified drivers/day assumption is too slow for the market window. With PJ + 5 agents + aggressive systems, we target ~1,000 drivers/day at peak recruitment in Q1, then ramp down as focus shifts to rider acquisition and retention.

Rapid Onboarding System

Digital-first, human-touch fallback: NIC + license upload via WhatsApp or in-app. QR codes at stands linking to instant signup. Agents call drivers personally if they stall during signup. Stand-by-stand blitz: 5 agents covering different zones, 5 stands/day per agent × 200 contacts/day = 1,000 contacts/day, ~50% conversion = 500 new signups/day (accounting for verification failures). Ramping to 1,000/day in M2-M3 with incentives and referral bonuses. Incentives: Priority placement in matching algorithm, founding driver badge, early payment bonus, referral bonuses (LKR 500-1,000 per successful referral). Verification turnaround: Same-day or next-day for agents to close the gap.

Timeline: M1: 100-200/day. M2: 500-700/day. M3: 1,000/day peak. By end of R1, target 500+ verified, active drivers ready for public launch in M4.

Revenue Model

Zero Commission, Forever — Revenue From Tiered Subscriptions

WarenYan will never charge drivers commission. Revenue comes from a four-tier subscription model where every paid feature is designed to increase the driver's earning potential. Every feature is opt-in, and free-tier drivers get the complete ride-hailing experience at zero cost forever. Minimum Base Subscription Charge — The Floor Price

Working backwards from operational costs: at $5,500/month ops (320 LKR/USD = LKR 1,760,000), with 20% combined conversion at 5,000 drivers (= 1,000 paying subscribers), the minimum average subscription price to break even is LKR 1,760/month. With 25% buffer: LKR 2,200/month.

The blended average across tiers achieves this: (WarenYan+ at LKR 99 × 40%) + (Pro at LKR 990 × 18%) + (Elite at LKR 2,500 × 5%) = weighted average ~LKR 343/driver/month across all drivers. This means break-even requires ~5,100 drivers at current conversion assumptions — achievable in Month 10-12.

Mid ($5.5K) $5,500 — ~5,100 drivers ~3,400 drivers
Full ($7K) $7,000 — ~6,500 drivers ~4,300 drivers

Break-even on operational costs: Full monthly operational cost at scale is ~$5,500-7,000 (including CRM, CS, call centre, insurance, analytics — not just tech stack). At 2,000 drivers (launch), subscription revenue covers ~20-25% of costs. At 5,000 drivers (Month 10-12), revenue reaches ~97% of mid-range costs. At 7,500+ drivers, the platform self-sustains. This plan does not assume profitability at launch — it assumes Series A readiness, with the subscription model proving conversion rates and unit economics that justify scale funding.

The LKR 99 entry tier is not designed to carry revenue load. It exists to (1) create paying customer relationships for investor narrative, (2) test payment infrastructure, (3) seed the upgrade funnel to Pro/Elite, and (4) provide instant branding ("WarenYan+ for ninety-nine rupees"). At 40% conversion of 5,000 drivers, it produces ~$619/month — modest revenue but 2,000 paying subscribers on the books.

At $100K deployed and 2,000 drivers, our full monthly operational cost is ~$5,500-7,000/month (including CRM, CS infrastructure, Google Workspace, call centre, insurance, analytics — everything). PickMe spends an estimated $50,000+/month on infrastructure alone. We run a complete operation for 7-10x less. Our cost structure makes zero-commission sustainable at any scale. The $100K doesn't change the thesis — it proves it works in the real world with real people, with a team executing ground operations at speed.

Glossary

Key Terms & Abbreviations

PVT LTD
Private Limited Company — standard Sri Lankan business entity registration under the Companies Act.
LKR
Sri Lankan Rupee — national currency. Plan uses 350 LKR/USD as conservative buffer (spot ~300).
PJ
Prageeth Jayathilaka — DGTL business partner executing all on-ground operations for WarenYan. Contract role, salary ramps LKR 100K→500K on milestone success.
Flutter
Google's cross-platform mobile framework — builds Android + iOS from single codebase. Used for WarenYan native app development ($30K/quarter flat rate).
PWA
Progressive Web App — web application that works like a native app. WarenYan's current MVP runs as a PWA; Flutter native app is the upgrade path.
PostGIS
Spatial database extension for PostgreSQL — powers WarenYan's real-time ride matching using st_dwithin proximity queries. Same technology Uber uses.
Supabase
Open-source Firebase alternative — provides database (PostgreSQL), authentication, real-time subscriptions, and Edge Functions. WarenYan's backend infrastructure.
PDPA
Personal Data Protection Act — Sri Lanka's data protection legislation (modelled on GDPR). WarenYan's privacy framework is PDPA-aligned.
GDPR
General Data Protection Regulation — EU data protection law. Referenced as the international standard WarenYan's data practices align with.
NIC
National Identity Card — Sri Lankan government-issued ID. Required for driver verification (NIC + driving license + vehicle registration).
UGC
User-Generated Content — authentic content created by drivers (testimonials, day-in-the-life videos). Core to WarenYan's emotional-anarchist marketing strategy.
SOS
Emergency alert system — one-tap safety feature with GPS breadcrumbs every 5 seconds. Live from Day 1 in WarenYan.
RBAC
Role-Based Access Control — permission system in Owner Hub (Owner/Admin/Agent roles). Controls who can view/manage what.
FCM
Firebase Cloud Messaging — Google's push notification service. Used for ride request alerts and status updates in the native app.
TWA
Trusted Web Activity — wraps a PWA in a native Android shell for Google Play distribution. Fallback if Flutter app development slips.
IEA
International Energy Agency — provides global oil price forecasts referenced in geopolitical buffer assumptions.
OPEC
Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries — oil cartel whose production decisions affect Sri Lanka's fuel costs and driver economics.
Series A
First significant round of venture capital financing. WarenYan targets $500K-$1M Series A in Q4 2027 after proving traction with seed funding.
DGTL
Digital services company — Riz Razak's existing business entity. PJ is a DGTL business partner contracted for WarenYan ground operations.
