Section 01

The Arrest That Shook Sri Lanka

At dawn on 26 February 2026, CID officers arrested the man at the centre of the darkest conspiracy in Sri Lankan history.

Breaking — 26 February 2026

Retired Major General Suresh Sallay was arrested at his residence in Peliyagoda by the CID. He is being held under the Prevention of Terrorism Act (PTA) on charges of conspiracy and aiding and abetting the Easter Sunday attacks. Under the PTA, he may be detained for up to 90 days without being produced before a court.

Why This Matters

This is the single most high-profile arrest in seven years of investigation into the 2019 Easter Sunday bombings that killed 269 people, including 45 foreigners. The arrest comes just before the seventh anniversary and fulfils a central campaign promise of President Anura Kumara Dissanayake. Sallay is not peripheral — he is the former Director of the State Intelligence Service and the man whom whistleblowers allege personally met the bombers and permitted the attack to proceed.

Section 02

Who Is Suresh Sallay?

A career military intelligence officer who rose to lead Sri Lanka's premier spy agency — and now stands accused of conspiracy in mass murder.

Career Profile

Sallay served in the Directorate of Military Intelligence (DMI) during and after Sri Lanka's civil war. He was employed in the unit that originally funded the NTJ — confirmed by a former NTJ member and admitted by the government. After Gotabaya Rajapaksa won the presidency in November 2019, Sallay was promoted to SIS Director. Dismissed by President Dissanayake in October 2024. Previously removed from a posting for alleged links to military-backed gangs in Jaffna.

The Core Allegation

Channel 4's 2023 documentary cited whistleblower Hanzeer Azad Maulana, who alleged he brokered a 2018 meeting between NTJ members and Sallay at Pillayan's direction. Maulana claims Sallay said "creating national insecurity was the only way to return the Rajapaksa family to power." After seeing CCTV of the bombers, Maulana says he recognised them from the meeting. Sallay categorically denies all allegations.

Section 03

Zahran Hashim: The Full Story

From a poor boy in Kattankudy to the mastermind of Sri Lanka's worst atrocity — and the deeply troubling questions about who was pulling his strings.

Early Life & Family

Mohammed Zahran Hashim was born into a poor Muslim family in Kattankudy, Batticaloa district. His father sold food packets on the street and reportedly engaged in petty theft, earning the nickname 'Kallan Hashim'. The family placed their hopes on Zahran, who joined the Jamiathul Falah Arabic College at age 12. For three years he memorised the Quran with remarkable aptitude, but by 15 was arguing his teachers were "too liberal." He was expelled in 2007 for insubordination and radical views.

The Intelligence Nexus: What Doesn't Add Up

Confirmed: DMI funded the NTJ

Cabinet spokesman Dr. Rajitha Senaratne disclosed that at least 26 NTJ members had been on the payroll of military intelligence, linked to Gotabaya Rajapaksa's intelligence apparatus. The Easter Commission report confirms weapons training by intel operatives 'Amy Mohideen' and 'Faiz'. A mysterious figure 'Jameel' connected to both military intelligence and the Easter attack plot.

The Central Question

Was Zahran a genuine organic extremist who was monitored and funded by intelligence, or was he — at least in part — a creature of the intelligence apparatus, groomed and facilitated by actors who planned to deploy him as a weapon? The answer likely lies between these poles: a genuine radical identified, infiltrated, funded, and potentially manipulated by intelligence actors with their own political agenda.

Section 04

NTJ Origins & the Thowheed Movement

Tracing the ideological pipeline from Saudi Wahhabism through the SLTJ to the NTJ's murderous endgame.

Ideological Roots

The NTJ emerged from the broader Thowheed movement, influenced by Saudi Wahhabism. The parent SLTJ was not violent but created the ideological substrate for radical offshoots. Kattankudy — with its high concentration of madrassas and a historically marginalised Muslim community — became the epicentre.

The ISIS Connection

ISIS claimed responsibility via Amaq two days after the attacks. Zahran pledged allegiance to al-Baghdadi and attempted to establish "Willayath-As-Seylani" as an IS province. However, investigators found no direct foreign operational link. The FBI noted the attackers were "members and supporters of ISIS" who created "ISIS in Sri Lanka," but operational planning was domestic. Contacts with IS followers in Tamil Nadu and Kerala provided ideological reinforcement. Naufer Moulavi reportedly brokered the ISIS connection.
