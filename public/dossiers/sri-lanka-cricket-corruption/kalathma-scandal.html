#KalathmaGate
In early 2023, a young Sri Lankan TikTok creator ‚Äî then just 17 years old, a teenager ‚Äî posted a video simply asking her followers to share celebrity stories. The elite deployed defamation law ‚Äî a mechanism built for corporations and public figures ‚Äî against a teenager with a phone and a TikTok account (Kalathma has since turned 18, in November 2025). Not one person named in the 20,000 comments addressed the substance of what was said. That tells you everything.

Religious Influence The Born Again / Glorious Church Network

A five-member investigative committee headed by retired Supreme Court Judge Sarojini Kusala Weerawardena produced a 63-page report (January 2023) commissioned by Sports Minister Roshan Ranasinghe. The report documented the influence of Prophet Jerome Fernando's Glorious Church ("Born Again" sect) on player selections, coaching appointments, and administrative decisions within SLC. Witnesses were so intimidated they refused to testify fully to a sitting judicial committee ‚Äî itself an extraordinary finding.

Sexual Misconduct The DM Scandal & Online Predation

Multiple credible reports and a viral social media thread document a pattern of Sri Lanka players ‚Äî some married, some in long-term relationships ‚Äî targeting models, influencers, and women online. The most extreme case resulted in a criminal arrest in Sydney. A second hotel-room incident in Galle was confirmed by SLC before being quietly suppressed. The TikTok thread opened the floodgates for dozens of similar allegations.

‚úì CONFIRMED ‚Äî Court Record
Danushka Gunathilaka
Batsman ¬∑ National XI ¬∑ Active at time of arrest
Arrested Sexual Assault Acquitted Tinder / Dating App
What happened
During the T20 World Cup, Sydney, November 2022: Gunathilaka matched with a woman on a dating app. They met at the Opera Bar, had dinner in the CBD, then took a ferry to her eastern suburbs home. She subsequently alleged non-consensual acts including non-consensual choking, stealthing (removing condom without consent), and digital penetration. Police arrested him from the Hyatt Regency Hotel as the rest of the Sri Lanka squad were preparing to depart.
The charges
Originally 4 counts of sexual assault. Three were dropped by prosecutors before trial. One count of non-consensual intercourse proceeded. Judge-alone trial at Downing Centre District Court (Judge Sarah Huggett). Four-day trial.
The verdict
Not guilty ‚Äî September 2023. Judge found "no opportunity" for the alleged stealthing given the continuous nature of intercourse. Noted police handling was "very concerning" and "far from satisfactory" ‚Äî officers threw out notes, omitted details, and potentially contaminated witnesses. Complainant deemed credible but at times appeared to be "motivated by a desire to paint the cricketer in an unfavourable light."
Prior misconduct (2021)
April 2021: SLC censured Gunathilaka for a brawl at a hotel event in Matara. Three-member inquiry panel found his conduct "unprofessional and lacking integrity." Directed to attend mandatory counselling for one year. He was also one of three players (with Dickwella and Mendis) given a 1-year ban (later reduced to 6 months) for breaching COVID protocols in England 2021 ‚Äî caught on video socialising with women outside the team bubble.

All three received 1-year bans from all international cricket. Bans later reduced to 6 months on appeal. ‚úì CONFIRMED ‚Äî SLC Official Statement
Niroshan Dickwella
Wicket-Keeper Batsman ¬∑ National XI
Cocaine Positive WADA-Banned Substance LPL 2024 Potential 4-year ban
What happened
During the Lanka Premier League 2024, the Sri Lanka Anti-Doping Agency (SLADA) conducted a test on Dickwella as part of SLC's anti-doping programme (run in collaboration with the Ministry of Sports under WADA guidelines). Dickwella's sample returned a positive result for cocaine ‚Äî a prohibited narcotic under the WADA Prohibited List. SLC publicly announced his suspension on August 16, 2024.
Consequences
Faces a potential 4-year ban from all forms of sport if found guilty. Cocaine is also a criminal narcotic under Sri Lankan law ‚Äî meaning he could face criminal prosecution separate from any sporting sanction. This is not a performance-enhancing substance; it is a recreational drug.
Pattern
Dickwella was previously one of the three players banned for COVID protocol breaches in England 2021 (socialising with women outside the bubble). The Sunday Times Sri Lanka described the pattern as "a glaring reminder of a troubling trend ‚Äî the steady erosion of discipline and integrity" in SLC.
Status: Suspended. Under adjudication. SLC has confirmed the positive test publicly. Outcome of formal anti-doping hearing pending.
‚úì CONFIRMED ‚Äî ICC / WADA
Kusal Perera
Wicket-Keeper Batsman ¬∑ National XI
Steroid Allegation Cleared / Acquitted WADA Lab Error ‚Äî Qatar
What happened
In November 2015, the WADA-accredited laboratory in Qatar reported that Perera's urine samples tested positive for 19-Norandrostenedione, an anabolic steroid. He was provisionally suspended by the ICC.
Outcome
Cleared ‚Äî 2016. The ICC withdrew all disciplinary charges after the Qatar laboratory withdrew its own Adverse Analytical Finding. ICC stated: "There is no evidence that Mr. Perera has ever used performance-enhancing substances." Laboratory error ‚Äî not a genuine doping case.
