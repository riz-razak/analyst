Executive Summary

In early 2026, nine shipments of substandard South African coal delivered to the Lakvijaya power plant exposed a Rs. 9 billion loss to the Ceylon Electricity Board — becoming the defining test of whether Sri Lanka's NPP government can deliver on its foundational promise of anti-corruption governance. The scandal implicates Energy Minister Kumara Jayakody (now indicted), reveals systematic tender manipulation, and threatens power cuts through July 2026. Simultaneously, the US-Israel strikes on Iran (Feb 28, 2026) have pushed Brent crude past $119/barrel, effectively closed the Strait of Hormuz, and placed 1.5 million Sri Lankan Gulf workers and $8.1 billion in annual remittances at risk — compounding a crisis that could derail Sri Lanka's fragile post-2022 recovery.

04 Iran War: Impact on Sri Lanka

On February 28, 2026, coordinated US-Israel strikes killed Iran's Supreme Leader — triggering the most severe energy disruption in decades. For Sri Lanka, still recovering from 2022's collapse, the timing could not be worse.

Hormuz — 90% Traffic Collapse

20% of global oil through Hormuz. Ships divert via Cape (+10-14 days). War risk +400%. Tea exports losing $10-15M/week. Surcharges $1,500-3,500/container.

$8.1B Remittances at Risk

1.5M Sri Lankans in Gulf (92% of migrants). Record $8.1B (2025) covers ~80% of trade deficit. If evacuations (1990 precedent), loss is catastrophic. Over 1/3 are female domestic workers.

Zero wheat produced domestically — 1.2M tonnes imported. 39% of households face inadequate diets. Oil→transport→food price cascade threatens deflationary reversal.

// ── OIL COST MODEL ──
// Sri Lanka imports ~11.1M bbl/year ($3.83B at $70)
// Each $1 above baseline × annualised weekly = additional cost
oilDelta = oil - 70  // $/bbl above pre-crisis
weeklyOilCost = (oilDelta × 11.1M / 52) × duration
// Source: CBSL Annual Report 2024, EconomyNext

// ── SHIPPING COST MULTIPLIER ──
// Hormuz carries ~20% global oil; closure forces Cape detour
// +10-14 days transit, war risk premium ~400%
shippingMultiplier = 1 + ((100 - hormuzTraffic) / 100) × 1.5
// Source: FT.lk "When Two Chokepoints Close"

// ── TEA EXPORT LOSS ──
// $10-15M/week lost when Hormuz traffic < 50%
teaLoss = (100-hor) > 50 ? 12.5×dur : (100-hor)/100 × 15×dur
// Source: Daily Mirror, Sri Lanka Tea Board

// ── REMITTANCE LOSS ──
// Base: $8,076M/year (2025 record)
// 1.5M Sri Lankans in Gulf = 92% of migrant workers
weeklyRemitLoss = ((8076 - 8076×(rem/100)) / 52) × dur
// Source: CBSL, MEI Gulf Migration Study

// ── DIESEL BACKUP COST ──
// When Lakvijaya underperforms, diesel backup at oil price
// Capacity deficit × oil-indexed cost × hours × weeks
coalDeficit = 810 - coal  // MW below rated capacity
dieselCost = coalDeficit × (oil × 0.15) × 7 × dur / 1000
// Source: CEB Generation Plan, PUCSL Reports

// ── RESERVE DRAIN ──
totalDrain = oilCost + remitLoss + dieselCost + teaLoss
reservesRemaining = max(0, 6800 - totalDrain)  // $6.8B baseline
monthsCover = reservesRemaining / 2000  // ~$2B/month import
// Source: CBSL External Sector Reports

// ── INFLATION ESTIMATE ──
// Simplified: weighted sum of oil, shipping, remittance shocks
inflation = (oilDelta/50)×3 + ((100-hor)/100)×2 + ((100-rem)/100)×1.5
// NOT a CPI model. Directional only.

// ── POWER CUT PROBABILITY ──
powerCut = min(95%, max(5%,
  (coalDeficit/810)×60         // coal underperformance weight
  + (dur>8 ? 20 : dur>4 ? 10 : 0)  // duration escalation
  + (oil>120 ? 15 : oil>100 ? 8 : 0) // diesel cost deterrent
))
// Source: PUCSL Apr-Jul warning, CEB data

  40                          // lowered baseline (was 55)
  - 0.7 × 25 × 0.25          // scandal severity (fixed: coal)
  - econPain × 30 × 0.30     // economic performance
  - (powerCut/100) × 20 × 0.20  // public sentiment proxy
  - duration × 0.8            // time decay of support
))
oppositionMomentum = min(85%, 100 - nppApproval + 5)
// Source: Author's model. NOT a poll.

  + (dur>12 ? 15 : dur>6 ? 8 : 0)
))
imfRisk = min(80%, max(5%,
  econPain × 40
  + (monthsCover<2 ? 30 : monthsCover<3 ? 15 : 0)
  + (dur>12 ? 10 : 0)
))
// Source: IMF Fourth Review, Sovereign Risk Framework
